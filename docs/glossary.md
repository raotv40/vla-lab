# Glossary of Robotics & Embodied AI Terms

This living glossary compiles terms and concepts learned during the VLA Learning Lab curriculum, sorted by the day they were first introduced.

| Term | Definition | First Learned | Last Reviewed | Confidence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Action** | A command generated by the robot's controller or policy that changes the robot's behavior. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ |
| **Action Space** | The set of all valid actions that the robot is allowed to perform. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **End-Effector** | The endpoint of a robot arm (for example, a fingertip or gripper) that interacts directly with the environment. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ |
| **Environment** | The virtual world in which a robot operates. It defines the robot, objects, physics, rewards, observations, and episode rules. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Episode** | One complete interaction between the robot and the environment, starting with `env.reset()` and ending when the task is completed, terminated, or truncated. | Day 1 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Gymnasium** | A Python framework that provides standardized environments for reinforcement learning experiments. It manages episodes, observations, actions, and rewards. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Joint** | A movable connection between robot links that allows rotational or translational motion. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ |
| **mjData** | A dynamic C structure in MuJoCo representing current physical states (positions, velocities, forces, simulation time). | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **mjModel** | A static C structure in MuJoCo containing physical properties (geoms, masses, joint types, limits) that remain unchanged. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **MuJoCo** | A high-performance physics simulator used to model robots and their interactions with the environment. Widely used in robotics and reinforcement learning research. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Observation** | The information actually available to the robot at a given moment. It represents what the robot currently perceives about itself and its surroundings. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Observation Space** | The set of all possible observations that an environment can provide to the robot. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Passive Simulation** | Simulating a physics model without active actuators (e.g., a ball falling under gravity or a free swinging arm). | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Render** | The process of displaying the simulation visually so that the user can observe the robot interacting with the environment. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Reset** | A function (`env.reset()`) that starts a new episode by initializing the environment and returning the first observation. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Robot Arm (Manipulator)** | A robotic mechanism with multiple joints used to reach, grasp, or manipulate objects. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Active Simulation** | Simulating a model where actuators actively apply forces or torques computed by a control policy. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Closed-Loop Control** | A feedback control system where the controller reads state observations to compute and adjust control actions. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Forward Kinematics** | Calculating the Cartesian position $(x, y)$ of the end-effector (fingertip) given the joint angles $(\theta_1, \theta_2)$. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Jacobian Matrix** | A matrix of first-order partial derivatives relating joint velocities to linear/angular velocities of the end-effector. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Policy** | The robot's decision-making function that maps observations to actions, determining what the robot should do next. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐☆☆☆ |
| **Sensor** | A device that converts physical information into observations. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **State** | The complete description of the environment, including information the robot may not directly access. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐☆☆☆ |
| **State Space** | The set of all possible states of the environment. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐☆☆☆ |
| **Torque Control** | Commanding rotational force (torque) to joints, requiring the solver to calculate acceleration and velocity. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Transpose Jacobian Control** | Mapping Cartesian forces to joint torques using the transpose of the Jacobian ($\tau = J^T F$) for target reaching. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Return** | The cumulative sum of rewards collected by the agent over the course of a single episode. | Day 3 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Reward** | A scalar feedback signal returned by the environment at each step representing the immediate utility of the transition. | Day 3 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Termination** | The condition where an episode ends naturally because the goal was reached or a failure state was entered. | Day 3 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Transition** | A single step transition containing $(s_t, a_t, r_t, s_{t+1})$, representing the core unit of interaction in MDPs. | Day 3 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Truncation** | The condition where an episode ends due to an artificial time-limit or step budget constraint. | Day 3 | Day 3 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
