# 📚 VLA Learning Lab Glossary

> A living glossary that grows throughout the 90-Day Vision-Language-Action Learning Journey.

| Term | Definition | First Learned | Last Reviewed | Confidence |
|------|------------|:-------------:|:-------------:|:----------:|
| **MuJoCo** | A high-performance physics simulator used to model robots and their interactions with the environment. Widely used in robotics and reinforcement learning research. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Gymnasium** | A Python framework that provides standardized environments for reinforcement learning experiments. It manages episodes, observations, actions, and rewards. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Environment** | The virtual world in which a robot operates. It defines the robot, objects, physics, rewards, observations, and episode rules. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Episode** | One complete interaction between the robot and the environment, starting with `env.reset()` and ending when the task is completed or terminated. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Observation** | The information available to the robot at a specific moment. It represents what the robot currently perceives about itself and its surroundings. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Observation Space** | The set of all possible observations that an environment can provide to the robot. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Action** | A command generated by the robot's controller or policy that changes the robot's behavior. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ |
| **Action Space** | The set of all valid actions that the robot is allowed to perform. | Day 1 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Simulation** | A virtual recreation of the physical world that allows robots to be tested safely before deployment on real hardware. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Reset** | A function (`env.reset()`) that starts a new episode by initializing the environment and returning the first observation. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| **Render** | The process of displaying the simulation visually so that the user can observe the robot interacting with the environment. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Robot Arm (Manipulator)** | A robotic mechanism with multiple joints used to reach, grasp, or manipulate objects. The Reacher environment is a simple example of a robotic arm. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ |
| **Joint** | A movable connection between robot links that allows rotational or translational motion. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ |
| **End-Effector** | The endpoint of a robot arm (for example, a gripper or tool) that interacts directly with the environment. | Day 1 | Day 1 | ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ || Term | Definition | First Learned | Last Reviewed | Confidence |
|------|------------|:-------------:|:-------------:|:----------:|
| **Policy** | The robot's **brain**—a decision-making function that maps observations (or states) to actions, determining what the robot should do next. In modern AI systems, policies are typically implemented as neural networks. | Day 2 | Day 2 | ⭐⭐☆☆☆ |
